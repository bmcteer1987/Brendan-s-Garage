Step-by-step guide for getting a repair covered under your A-Protect warranty. Tap each step to mark it complete.

1
Confirm the issue is a covered component
A-Protect Diamond Plus covers most mechanical and electrical failures. Review your contract for exclusions -- wear items like brake pads, tires, and glass are typically excluded. Engine, transmission, cooling, electrical, and A/C are usually included. Maximum payout is $7,500 per claim.
○
2
Do not authorize any repairs yet
Before any work begins, A-Protect must pre-authorize the claim. Authorizing repairs without their approval almost always voids coverage. Drive or tow to a shop that will hold work pending authorization.
○
3
Call A-Protect's claims line
1-800-265-8699 -- have your warranty contract number, VIN, current mileage, and a brief description of the issue ready. Open Mon–Fri 8am–5pm EST. After hours, leave a message and do not proceed with repairs.
○
4
Take the car to a licensed repair facility
A-Protect requires a licensed shop (most reputable shops qualify). If you have a preferred BMW specialist, confirm they'll work with A-Protect claims before dropping the car off -- most will, but call ahead.
○
5
Mechanic contacts A-Protect for pre-authorization
The shop calls A-Protect directly (same number) with a repair estimate and diagnostic codes. A-Protect may send an inspector for larger claims -- more likely given the $7,500 cap makes every claim meaningful. Authorization typically takes a few hours to one business day.
○
6
Get the authorization number in writing
Before work starts, ensure both you and the shop have the A-Protect authorization number. This is your reference if there's a billing dispute later. Screenshot or photograph any confirmation you receive.
○
7
Pay your deductible; A-Protect covers the rest
Your deductible is listed on your contract. A-Protect pays the shop directly for the approved amount, up to $7,500 per claim. Keep your itemized invoice regardless.
○
8
Log the repair in the Tracker
Record the repair date, mileage, shop, cost, and authorization number in the Tracker tab. Useful if a related issue surfaces later or when you sell the car.
○
Key rule: Never pay out of pocket expecting reimbursement. A-Protect requires pre-authorization before work begins -- retroactive claims are almost never approved. Also note the $7,500 per-claim cap: get a full itemized estimate before authorizing so you know your exposure.

B58 engine + 6-speed manual -- items to monitor on the M240i
Engine oil -- LL-01 spec only
BMW uses extended intervals but used cars may be overdue. Use only LL-01 approved 0W-30 or 5W-30. The B58 is sensitive to incorrect oil spec -- it matters more than most engines.
Every 15,000–25,000 km or annually
Valve cover / oil separator (VANOS)
B58 engines can develop oil separator and valve cover gasket weeping. Look for oily residue at the top of the engine. A cold-start VANOS rattle is worth monitoring and documenting.
Inspect at every oil change
Coolant expansion tank
Plastic coolant tanks on BMWs are known to crack. Inspect for hairline cracks or discoloration, and watch for a sweet smell or low coolant warnings -- early signs of a weeping tank.
Inspect every 6 months; flush every 4 years
Gearbox oil -- 6-speed manual
The manual gearbox oil is often overlooked. BMW does not specify a change interval but most specialists recommend replacing it at 80,000 km or if the history is unknown. Use BMW-approved MTF (e.g. Pentosin MTF 5). A fresh fill makes a noticeable difference to shift feel.
Change if history unknown or >80k km
Brakes -- pads, rotors, fluid
M Sport brakes wear faster than average. Check pad thickness and rotor scoring regularly. The front brakes take significant load. Brake fluid absorbs moisture and should be flushed every two years regardless of mileage.
Inspect pads every 15,000 km; fluid every 2 years
Battery (AGM) and coding
BMW requires battery coding after replacement -- a new battery without coding causes charging issues and shortened lifespan. If the battery is original and over 4–5 years old, test it proactively before a cold Toronto winter.
Test annually if >3 years old
Rear differential, CV boots and prop shaft
RWD only -- no front diff. Inspect the rear half-shaft CV boots for tears (grease flung around the axle = failed boot). Check the rear diff for leaks and consider a fluid change if history is unknown. Inspect the prop shaft flex disc (guibo) and centre bearing for cracks or play -- a worn guibo causes driveline shudder.
CV boots: inspect annually · Rear diff fluid: change if unknown history
